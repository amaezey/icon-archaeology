Introduction

January 2002
Aqua Objects 1 by Gary Gehiere

As an icon designer, I love Aqua - I love the big icon space, the alpha channels, I love the whole Aqua metaphor.

What I don't like about aqua is all the shrunken-photos and badly rendered 3D graphics that are being passed as real Aqua icons.

So, when I made these icons I tried to follow Apple's guidelines as much as possible, specifically that icons should look photographic yet illustrated but not be photos or 3D rendered.

I learned alot making these and I look forward to learning more as I dive deeper into the new possibilties of Apple's new GUI - hopefully regaining my previous comfort and powress with icon design in the new OSX arena.

I should give credit where it's due - sometimes I draw inspiration from existing metaphors and icons becuase I'm striving for conformity between my work and some existing icons.

When I make a hard drive I want it to look good with the operating system's default hard drive icon, or maybe I want my document icon to look good next to the Iconfactory's document icon.

So if you think you see the influence of another designer in my work, you probably are correct.

However, I never copy any pixels - I've made all these icons myself!!

So as for my influences, the gradiants on the Macintosh Classic monitor are inspiredby an icon by the Iconfactory (which I can't seem to place at the time of this writing), the 'artwork' and 'correspondance' icons are very much inspired by those guys as well.

The Zip disks and drives are inspired from the MacOS itself, kinda... I couldn't find the Zip disk icon in the MacOS resources anywhere, so I made my own based on a small preview I found in a screenshot.

And of course, the 'aqua globe' wouldn't look so good without pixeljerk's helpful aqua button tips.

To other icon designers who might be reading this, I have a challenge; I challenge you to follow Apple's guidelines and refrain from using photos or 3D renderings as finished icon - let's make the after-market Aqua experience as beautiful and of the highest quality possible!!

Come on in - the water's fine ;) And to everyone, I hope you enjoy these icons and that they find a nice home on your Mac!
