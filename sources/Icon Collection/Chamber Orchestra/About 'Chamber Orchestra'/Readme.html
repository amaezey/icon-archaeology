Introduction

March 2002
Chamber Orchestra by Gary Gehiere

My wife Trina has frequently mentioned how great her desire is to study the cello again - she played for a few years in school but has had the cello absent from her life for almost 10 years.

Late last year she finally bought her own cello and arranged for lessons. As she has lubricated her skill with daily practise the squeeky sounds of rusty playing having turned into smooth timbres and gentle tone, much to the relief of my ears.

The physical beauty of the classical instruments are the inspiration for these icons - warm wooden colours and almost human curves of the instrument bodies.

I chose to limit myself to just the chamber instruments instead of attempting a full orchestra because I really felt no desire to delve into the complexity of so many of the classical instruments with their keys, valves and other mechanical workings. I aplogize for the omissions.

While I did work from source photos for these icons none of them are actually photographic - they are made completely by hand in Adobe Photoshop. However, vectorized sketches of the instrument bodies were drawn in Adobe Illustrator for the sake of accuracy.
